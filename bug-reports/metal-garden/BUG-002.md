# BUG-002 — Player Can Stand On or Clip Into Aquatic Enemy Model

| Status            | Confirmed                         |
| Reproduction Rate | Multiple successful reproductions |
| Category          | Collision / Hitbox                |
| Area           | Aquatic Zone — Enemy Encounter |

---

## Description

The player character can stand on top of the aquatic enemy or partially clip inside its 3D model. The enemy's collision boundaries do not accurately represent its visible mesh, allowing unintended spatial overlap between the player and the enemy.

---

## Steps to Reproduce

1. Enter the aquatic zone and locate an aquatic enemy.
2. Navigate the player character toward the top surface of the enemy model.
3. Attempt to stand on it or move into its model from the side.
4. Observe the player character overlapping with the enemy geometry.

---

## Expected Result

The player character should be blocked by the enemy's collision volume. No overlap between the player and the enemy model should be possible.

---

## Actual Result

The player character can stand on top of the enemy or partially clip into its mesh, indicating that the collision hitbox does not match the visual model geometry.

---

## Impact

Visually incorrect and inconsistent with expected physical interaction. The player can occupy a position inside an enemy model, which breaks visual fidelity.

> Note: No gameplay exploit, progression impact, or economy consequence is associated with this issue. The effect is purely cosmetic and spatial.

---

## Severity Justification

**Severity: Low** — The issue is a cosmetic and spatial inconsistency. It does not affect gameplay mechanics, progression, or player safety.

**Priority: Low** — Suitable for a collision polish pass. Does not block any feature or create a meaningful exploit.
