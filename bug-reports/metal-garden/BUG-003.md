# BUG-003 — Enemy Clips Into Wall During Dodge Animation

| ------------------ | -------------------------------- |
| ID                 | BUG-003                          |
| Game               | Metal Garden                     |
| Game Version       | 2.7.2                            |
| Date Reported      | 2026-06-11                       |
| Reported By        | Brendo de Araujo                 |
| Severity           | Medium                           |
| Priority           | Low                              |
| Status             | Needs Retest                     |
| Category           | Collision / AI Movement          |
| Area               | General — Enemy Encounter        |
| Reproduction Rate  | Observed once during gameplay    |

---

## Description

Some enemies perform a dodge reaction when the player gets close, jumping left, right, or toward nearby platforms. During one such dodge, the enemy entered a wall geometry and became embedded in it, disrupting the encounter.

---

## Steps to Reproduce

1. Locate an enemy with dodge behavior.
2. Approach the enemy closely to trigger its dodge reaction.
3. Observe the enemy's jump/dodge direction.
4. Check whether the dodge trajectory leads the enemy into a wall or piece of geometry.

---

## Expected Result

The enemy's dodge animation should be constrained by the environment's collision. The enemy should land on a valid surface or be redirected, never entering solid geometry.

---

## Actual Result

The enemy clipped into a wall during its dodge jump. Once inside the geometry, the enemy's behavior was disrupted and it could not function as intended for the remainder of the encounter.

---

## Impact

An enemy embedded in wall geometry may become unreachable or non-functional, trivializing the encounter without player intent. Depending on how frequently this occurs, it could be a consistent way to neutralize specific enemy types near walls.

---

## Severity Justification

**Severity: Medium** — The enemy becomes non-functional when clipped, breaking the intended encounter. The root cause likely lies in the dodge AI not validating the landing position against collision geometry.

**Priority: Low** — Observed once and requires retest to confirm reproducibility. Should be investigated but does not warrant immediate escalation.
